Full cold vertical displacement event (VDE) simulation of a major disruption in an ITER-like tokamak reactor

Zakariae Jorti (LANL), Qi Tang (LANL), Konstantin Lipnikov (LANL) and Xianzhu Tang (LANL)

LA-UR-23-21442. Approved for public release; distribution is unlimited.

Overview

We consider a multi-domain quasi-static MHD model for force-free plasmas: * In the plasma region: \begin{align} \frac{\partial n}{\partial t} + \nabla\cdot\left(n\mathbf{V}\perp\right) = & ~0 \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{P}, \label{eq:4.565} \ -\left( \nu n_0 m_i \nabla^2 \mathbf{V} + \dfrac{1}{\mu_0} (\nabla\times\mathbf{B})\times\mathbf{B} \right) \cdot \mathbf{e}R= & ~0 \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{P} , \label{eq:4.566} \ \mathbf{V}\perp \cdot \mathbf{B} = & ~ 0 \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{P}, \label{eq:4.567} \ -\left( \nu n_0 m_i \nabla^2 \mathbf{V}{\perp} + \dfrac{1}{\mu_0} (\nabla\times\mathbf{B})\times\mathbf{B} \right) \cdot \mathbf{e}_Z = & ~ 0 \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{P}, \label{eq:4.568} \ - \nabla^2 \Phi - \nabla\cdot\left[ - \mathbf{V}\perp\times \mathbf{B} + \frac{\eta}{\mu_0}\left(\nabla\times\mathbf{B}\right) \right] = & ~ 0 \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{P}, \label{eq:4.569} \ \boldsymbol{\tau} - \nabla\Phi + {\V}\perp\times \mathbf{B} - \frac{\eta}{\mu_0}\left(\nabla\times\mathbf{B}\right) = & ~ \mathbf{0} \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{P}, \label{eq:4.570} \ \frac{\partial\mathbf{B}}{\partial t} + \nabla\times\boldsymbol{\tau} = & ~ \mathbf{0} \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{P}. \label{eq:4.571} \end{align} * In the wall region: \begin{align} \frac{\partial n}{\partial t} = & ~ 0 \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{W}, \label{eq:4.572} \ \mathbf{V}\perp = & ~ \mathbf{0} \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{W} , \label{eq:4.573} \ - \nabla^2 \Phi - \nabla\cdot\left[ \frac{\eta}{\mu_0}\left(\nabla\times\mathbf{B}\right) \right] = & ~ 0 \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{W} \label{eq:4.574} \ \boldsymbol{\tau} - \nabla\Phi - \frac{\eta}{\mu_0}\left(\nabla\times\mathbf{B}\right) = & ~ \mathbf{0} \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{W}, \label{eq:4.575} \ \frac{\partial\mathbf{B}}{\partial t} + \nabla\times\boldsymbol{\tau} = & ~ \mathbf{0} \qquad \text{in} \quad \Omega^{W} . \label{eq:4.576} \end{align} * At the wall/plasma interface: \begin{alignat}{3} &\mathbf{V}\perp = ~ 0 \qquad && \text{on} \quad \Gamma^{PW} ,\label{eq:4.577} \ &\boldsymbol{\tau} \times \mathbf{n} \qquad && \text{continous across} \quad \Gamma^{PW}, \label{eq:4.579} \ &\mathbf{B} \cdot \mathbf{n} \qquad && \text{continous across} \quad \Gamma^{PW} . \label{eq:4.578} \end{alignat} * At the outer rectangular boundary $\partial \Omega$: \begin{align} \Phi & = ~ {0}, \ \boldsymbol{\tau} \times \mathbf{n} & = ~ \mathbf{0}. \end{align} where $n$ is the plasma density, $\mathbf{V}$ is the perpendicular flow field, $\mathbf{B}$ is the magnetic field, $\Phi$ is the electrostatic potential and $\boldsymbol{\tau}$ the divergence-free component of the electric field. $\nu$ is a viscosity coefficient, $\eta$ is the resistivity, $\mu_0$ is the vacuum magnetic permeability and $m_i$ is the mass of ions.

We develop a solver for the quasi-static force-free plasma model above. This solver is based on Mimetic Finite Difference (MFD) method and PETScâ€™s DMStag data structure for staggered grid representations, fully implicit, solved with the Jacobian-free Newton-Krylov and inexact Newton methods with a four-level field-split preconditioning strategy.

The MFD solver1 is used for simulating the cold vertical displacement events (VDE) of a major disruption in an ITER-like tokamak reactor over the actual plasma current diffusion time. The first movie shows the evolution of the toroidal current and magnetic field lines over time for isotropic resistivities inside the tokamak subdomains. On the other hand, the remaining animations correspond to a setting where the blanket module has an anisotropic resistivity (different values in toroidal and poloidal directions). This setting was employed to simulate the halo and eddy currents inside the tokamak.
